Briefly, this is what I do:

Coral reef ecology

Coral reefs are exceptional marine ecosystems, yet they are also threaten by recurrent natural and human-made disturbances. I am monitoring the responses of reefal communities (hard corals, soft corals, sponges, algae, etc) to these disturbances and try to predict how future reefs will look like. (I am a CPCe "enthousiast").

Traditional coral taxonomy

Scleractinian corals are the main engineer of modern coral reefs, yet identifying their species in situ is rock hard (seriously üòÖ). I am using a "traditional approach" based on skeletal microscopic observations to identify most corals to the species level (mainly for coral from the Indo-Pacific region)!

Molecular coral taxonomy

Scleractinian corals often look the same in situ but are genetically different. I am combining traditional and molecular approaches (mitochondrial and nuclear markers) to better identify species and assess molecular diversity ("each slight variation, if useful, is preserved") within natural populations.

Population genetics

Population genetics can be a very useful tool to understand past and present dynamics of scleractinian coral populations (breeding status, population size, connectivity level, etc). I use microsatellites markers to assess this dynamic and better predict how corals may recover (or not) following major disturbances.

Organismal performances

Changes in benthic communities is likely the end result of disturbances. Individuals may perform differently, adjusting their physiology or relying on their genetical background to better cope (or not) with disturbances. I am trying to assess this organism "performance" to better predict the community trajectory.

Field molecular ecologist

I see myself as an enthousiast field molecular ecologist, diver, with field experiences from many places around the world (Reunion Island, Taiwan, Palau, the Phillipines, Japan, etc). NITROX/TRIMIX certified, tech diver, familiar with molecular biology methods applied to ecology and still learning new things (like coding üòÅ)!
